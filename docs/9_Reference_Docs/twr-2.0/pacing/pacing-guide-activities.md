# Pacing Guide

## Grade K

### Sentences

#### Conduct all of these activities orally.
- Practice sentence expansion with **when**, **where**, and **why**.  
  Activities:  
  - Sentence Expansion (K–12)  
  - Kernel Expansion Scaffolds (K–12)  
  - Kernel Expansion for Sentences (K–12)

- Use the basic conjunctions **because** and **but**.  
  Activities:  
  - Because, But, So (K–12)

- Identify sentence types: statements and questions.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify the Sentence Type (K–12)

- Convert fragments into complete sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Fragments or Sentences? (1–12)  
    *(Note: Even though the pacing guide mentions this orally in K, the TWR activity “Fragments or Sentences?” is formally listed for Grades 1–12.)*

- Apply correct capitalization and end punctuation.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify the Sentence Type – Punctuation (1–12)  
    *(Again, introduced in Grade 1–12; K does it orally.)*

- Complete sentence stems beginning with **after** and **before**.  
  Activities: None  
  *(No exact TWR match for “after/before” at K.)*

  Activities: None

#### Conduct these activities using writing.
- Practice sentence expansion with all question words.  
  Activities:  
  - Sentence Expansion (K–12)  
  - Write Questions about a Picture (1–12) (related to question words)

- Use the basic conjunctions **because**, **but**, and **so**.  
  Activities:  
  - Because, But, So (K–12)

- Correct run-on sentences.  
  Activities:

  Activities:

  Activities:  
  - Complete Subordinating Conjunctions (1–12) *(They keep adding more conjunctions each year.)*

  Activities:  
  - Using Transition Words (2–12)

  Activities:  
  - Combining Sentences (2–12)

### Note-Taking
_(No items for this grade.)_

### SPO and Paragraph

#### Conduct these activities as a whole class.
- Practice creating SPOs and paragraphs.  
  Activities:  
  - Writing Single Paragraph Outlines (1–12)  
  - Turn Outline into Draft (2–12)

#### Conduct these additional activities
- Create a narrative SPO as a whole class, using time and sequence transitions.  
  Activities:  
  - Create SPO for narrative (3–12) *(Grade 2 might do it in a shared, introductory way.)*  
  - Using Transition Words (2–12)

- Brainstorm key words and phrases for a given topic sentence, then fill in an SPO as a whole class (for narrative and expository structures).  
  Activities:  
  - Writing Single Paragraph Outlines (1–12)  
  - Given T.S., Generate Details (2–12)

- Distinguish topic sentences from detail sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify Topic Sentence (2–12)  
  - Order Topic Sentence and Detail Sentences (2–12)  
  - Topic Sentence Matching (2–12)

- Generate a topic sentence from given detail sentences using different sentence types.  
  Activities:  
  - Given Details, Generate T.S. (2–12)  
  - Topic Sentence Improvement – Sentence Type (2–12)

- Select relevant details from a list to support a given topic sentence.  
  Activities:  
  - Eliminate Irrelevant Sentences (3–12) *(Primarily from Grade 3 on, but Grade 2 is starting the skill.*)  

### Revising and Editing

#### Conduct these activities as a whole class.
- Brainstorm more vivid or specific vocabulary for vague or overused words (e.g., fun, good).  
  Activities:  
  - Improve Vague Vocabulary (K–12)

- Revise an unelaborated paragraph with the entire class using explicit directions.  
  Activities:  
  - Elaborate on Paragraphs (2–12)

## Grade 3

### Sentences

- Change fragments into complete sentences.

  Activities:  
  - Unscramble Sentences (2–12)

  Activities:  
  - Identify the Sentence Type – Punctuation (1–12)

- Differentiate between sentence types.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify the Sentence Type (K–12)  
  - Change the Sentence Type (1–12)

- Practice sentence expansion.  
  Activities:  
  - Sentence Expansion (K–12)  
  - Kernel Expansion Scaffolds (K–12)  
  - Kernel Expansion for Sentences (K–12)

- Develop questions about content.  
  Activities:  
  - Write Questions about a Picture (1–12)

- Correct run-on sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify and Correct Run-on sentences (2–12)

- Use the basic conjunctions **because**, **but**, and **so**.  
  Activities:  
  - Because, But, So (K–12)

- Use the subordinating conjunctions **After**, **Before**, **If**, **When**, **Even though**, **Whenever**, **Although**, and **Since**.  
  Activities:  
  - Complete Subordinating Conjunctions (1–12)

- Combine two or three sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Combining Sentences (2–12)

- Add transitions for time and sequence (first, next, then, finally), conclusions (in conclusion, therefore), and illustrations (for example, for instance).  
  Activities:  
  - Using Transition Words (2–12)

- Practice appositives (introduce at the middle or toward the end of the year).  
  Activities:  
  - Identify Appositives (3–12)  
  - Brainstorm Appositives (3–12)  
  - Given an Appositive, Write a Sentence (3–12), etc.

### Note-Taking

- Write key words, phrases, and common abbreviations on dotted lines.  
  Activities: None specifically mention “on dotted lines,” but note-taking expansions can relate to:  
  - Underline key words / phrases in a sentence (3–12) *(similar skill, not identical)*

- Introduce symbols like **/**, **=**, **→**, **+**.  
  Activities: None

### SPO and Paragraph

- Create SPOs for the following text structures as a whole class:  
  - **Narrative**  
  - **Opinion**  
  - **Expository**  
  Activities:  
  - Create SPO for narrative (3–12)  
  - Create SPO for opinion (3–12)  
  - Create SPO for expository (3–5)

- Practice SPO scaffolding activities:  
  1. Create an SPO with the whole class.  
  2. Distinguish a Topic Sentence (T.S.) from details.  
  3. Identify the T.S. and sequence details.  
  4. Given a T.S., select relevant details and eliminate irrelevant detail(s) from a list or SPO.  
  5. Given a T.S., generate details.  
  6. Practice two or three ways of writing T.S. or C.S. (if appositives introduced).  
  7. Given topic/prompt, generate a T.S.  
  8. Given a T.S. and details, generate a C.S.  
  9. Given topic/prompt, construct an SPO independently.  
  10. Draft a paragraph based on an SPO.  

  Activities:  
  - Writing Single Paragraph Outlines (1–12)  
  - Identify Topic Sentence (2–12)  
  - Given T.S., Generate Details (2–12)  
  - Eliminate Irrelevant Sentences (3–12)  
  - Practice two or three ways of writing T.S. or C.S. → (Topic Sentence Improvement – Sentence Type (2–12), Write a Topic Sentence Using Sentence Strategies (2–12))  
  - Given a topic/prompt, generate a T.S. → (Make Topic Sentences for Topic (2–12))  
  - Given a T.S. and details, generate a C.S. → (Given T.S. and Details, Generate C.S. (3–12))  
  - Turn Outline into Draft (2–12)

### Revising and Editing

- Brainstorm more specific and vivid vocabulary to replace vague or overused words.  
  Activities:  
  - Improve Vague Vocabulary (K–12)

- Improve given topic and concluding sentences by making them more specific and engaging.  
  Activities:  
  - Improve Topic & Concluding Sentences (3–12)  
  - Topic Sentence Improvement Brainstorm (3–12)

- Revise unelaborated paragraphs using explicit instructions, first as a class activity and then in pairs or small groups.  
  Activities:  
  - Elaborate on Paragraphs (2–12)

- Revise drafts based on explicit feedback from teachers and peers.  
  Activities:  
  - Revise and edit a draft from feedback (3–12)

- Proofread and edit writing for proper use of:  
  - Commas  
  - Capitalization  
  - Punctuation  
  - Complete sentences (fixing fragments and run-ons)  
  - Spelling  
  Activities:  
  - Identify and Correct Errors (1–12)

### TO, MPO, and Compositions

- Change fragments to sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Fragments or Sentences? (1–12)

- Unscramble sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Unscramble Sentences (2–12)

- Differentiate between sentence types.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify the Sentence Type (K–12)  
  - Change the Sentence Type (1–12)

- Practice sentence expansion.  
  Activities:  
  - Sentence Expansion (K–12)

- Develop questions.  
  Activities:  
  - Write Questions about a Picture (1–12)

- Use the basic conjunctions because, but, and so.  
  Activities:  
  - Because, But, So (K–12)

- Use the subordinating conjunctions After, Before, If, When, Even though, Whenever, Although, Since, While, and Unless.  
  Activities:  
  - Complete Subordinating Conjunctions (1–12)

- Combine three to five sentences.  
  Activities:  
  - Combining Sentences (2–12)

- Use transitions.  
  Activities:  
  - Using Transition Words (2–12)

- Use appositives.  
  Activities:  
  - Identify Appositives (3–12)
  - Brainstorm Appositives (3–12)

### Note-Taking

- Write key words, phrases, common abbreviations, and symbols on dotted lines.  
  Activities: None

### SPO and Paragraph

- Create SPOs for:  
  - Narrative  
  - Opinion  
  - Problem/solution  
  - Cause/effect  

  Activities:  
  - Create SPO for narrative (3–12)  
  - Create SPO for opinion (3–12)  
  - Create SPO for problem/solution (4–12)  
  - Create SPO for cause/effect (4–12)

- Create separate pro SPOs and con SPOs.  
  Activities:  
  - Create SPO for pro/con (4–12)

- Use SPO scaffolding:  
  1. Create an SPO as a whole class.  
  2. Distinguish a T.S. from details.  
  3. Identify T.S. and sequence details.  
  4. Given a T.S., select relevant details from a list.  
  5. Eliminate irrelevant detail(s) from a list or SPO.  
  6. Given a T.S., generate details.  
  7. Practice the three strategies for writing T.S. or C.S.  
  8. Given a topic/prompt, generate a T.S.  
  9. Given a T.S. and details, generate a C.S.  
  10. Given a topic/prompt, construct an SPO independently.

  Activities:  
  - Writing Single Paragraph Outlines (1–12)  
  - Identify Topic Sentence (2–12)  
  - Eliminate Irrelevant Sentences (3–12)  
  - Given T.S., Generate Details (2–12)  
  - Practice two or three ways of writing T.S. or C.S. → (Topic Sentence Improvement – Sentence Type (2–12))  
  - Make Topic Sentences for Topic (2–12)  
  - Given T.S. and Details, Generate C.S. (3–12)

- Draft a paragraph based on an SPO.  
  Activities:  
  - Turn Outline into Draft (2–12)

### Revising and Editing

- Use specific and varied vocabulary to replace overused or vague words.  
  Activities:  
  - Improve Vague Vocabulary (K–12)

- Improve given topic and concluding sentences using the three strategies.  
  Activities:  
  - Improve Topic & Concluding Sentences (3–12)  
  - Topic Sentence Improvement Brainstorm (3–12)

- Revise unelaborated paragraphs with explicit instructions, progressing to general instructions.  
  Activities:  
  - Elaborate on Paragraphs (2–12)

- Revise drafts with explicit instructions, progressing to general instructions.  
  Activities:  
  - Revise and edit a draft from feedback (3–12)

- Proofread and edit for:  
  - Commas  
  - Capitalization  
  - Punctuation  
  - Fragments  
  - Run-ons  
  - Spelling errors  
  - Verb tense  
  - Number agreement  

  Activities:  
  - Identify and Correct Errors (1–12)

- Apply sentence strategies when revising:  
  - Transitions  
  - Appositives  
  - Basic conjunctions  
  - Subordinating conjunctions  
  - Sentence expansion  

  Activities:  
  - Using Transition Words (2–12)  
  - Identify Appositives (3–12)  
  - Because, But, So (K–12)  
  - Complete Subordinating Conjunctions (1–12)  
  - Sentence Expansion (K–12)

- Cite evidence from text using:  
  - Illustration transitions  
  - Direct quotations  
  - Paraphrased quotations  

  Activities:  
  - Cite evidence with quotations (5–12)  
  - Incorporating quotations into a composition (5–12)

- Use Revise and Edit Checklist to check drafts.  
  Activities: None

  Activities:

### TO, MPO, and Compositions

- Develop a thesis statement for a TO as a whole class.  
  Activities:  
  - Create Thesis Statement for topic (4–12)  
  - Write a Thesis when given a topic (4–12)

- Create a TO as a whole class.  
  Activities:  
  - Create a draft from a TO (4–12)

- Given a thesis statement, fill in Main Idea boxes.  
  Activities:  
  - Generate Main Ideas from Thesis (6–12)  
  - Given a thesis statement, fill in the main idea boxes (6–12)

- Given a thesis statement, fill in Details boxes in note form.  
  Activities:  
  - Generate Details from Main idea & Thesis (6–12)  
  - Given a thesis statement, fill in the details boxes (6–12)

- Develop a draft from a TO.  
  Activities:  
  - Create a draft from a TO (4–12)

- Revise and edit a draft based on a TO.  
  Activities:  
  - Revise & edit a draft from a TO (4–6) *(6 is borderline, but listed in 4–6 range.)*

- Create a TO independently.  
  Activities:  
  - Create a draft from a TO (4–12) *(Implied that they now do it themselves.)*

- Create TOs for the following text structures:  
  - Narrative  
  - Problem/solution  
  - Compare and contrast  
  - Cause/effect  

  Activities:  
  - Create TOs for narrative (5–6) *(Primarily spelled out for 5–6, though 6 can do it as well.)*  
  - Create TOs for problem/solution (5–6)  
  - Create TOs for compare/contrast (5–6)  
  - Create TOs for cause/effect (5–6)

- Develop a neutral pro/con three- or four-paragraph TO and composition.  
  Activities:  
  - Create TOs for pro/con (5–6)

### Research Paper

- Introduce and practice the research paper process through teacher modeling and whole class activities, starting with biographical models.

#### Part 1: Background Research and Planning
- Read and annotate background articles  
- Gather and evaluate primary and secondary sources from academic databases  
- Identify and underline key words and phrases in sources  
- Create Works Cited page following proper citation format  
- Develop research plan and thesis statement  
- Take structured notes from sources using index cards  
- Organize and sequence index cards according to research plan  

Activities:  
- Conduct research on a topic (7–12)

#### Part 2: Writing and Development
- Convert index card notes into MPO details  
- Draft research paper introduction incorporating thesis  
- Transform MPO into complete research paper  

Activities:  
- Create a draft from an MPO (7–12)  
- Draft an introduction (7–12)  
- Revise & edit a draft from an MPO (7–12)

#### Research Paper Models
Practice the complete research process using these models:  
- Biographical research papers  
- Significant historical events  
- Problem/issue analysis  
- Argumentative research  
- Compare and contrast studies  

Activities:  
- Conduct research on a topic (7–12)  
- Draft, revise, & edit an argumentative composition from an MPO (7–12)

### Research Paper

- Introduce and practice the research paper process through teacher modeling and whole class activities, starting with biographical models.

#### Part 1: Background Research and Planning
- Read and annotate background articles  
- Gather and evaluate primary and secondary sources from academic databases  
- Identify and underline key words and phrases in sources  
- Create Works Cited page following proper citation format  
- Develop research plan and thesis statement  
- Take structured notes from sources using index cards  
- Organize and sequence index cards according to research plan  

Activities:  
- Conduct research on a topic (7–12)

#### Part 2: Writing and Development
- Convert index card notes into MPO details  
- Draft research paper introduction incorporating thesis  
- Transform MPO into complete research paper  

Activities:  
- Create a draft from an MPO (7–12)  
- Draft an introduction (7–12)  
- Revise & edit a draft from an MPO (7–12)

#### Research Paper Models
Practice the complete research process using these models:  
- Biographical research papers  
- Significant historical events  
- Problem/issue analysis  
- Argumentative research  
- Compare and contrast studies  

Activities:  
- Conduct research on a topic (7–12)  
- Draft, revise, & edit an argumentative composition from an MPO (7–12)
