- Unscramble sentences containing five or six words.

## Grade 3

### Sentences

- Change fragments into complete sentences.
- Unscramble sentences.
- Use correct capitalization and punctuation.
- Differentiate between sentence types.
- Practice sentence expansion.
- Develop questions about content.
- Correct run-on sentences.
- Use the basic conjunctions **because**, **but**, and **so**.
- Use the subordinating conjunctions **After**, **Before**, **If**, **When**, **Even though**, **Whenever**, **Although**, and **Since**.
- Combine two or three sentences.
- Add transitions for time and sequence (first, next, then, finally), conclusions (in conclusion, therefore), and illustrations (for example, for instance).
- Practice appositives (introduce at the middle or toward the end of the year).

### Note-Taking

- Write key words, phrases, and common abbreviations on dotted lines.
- Introduce symbols like **/**, **=**, **→**, **+**.

### SPO and Paragraph

- Create SPOs for the following text structures as a whole class:
  - **Narrative**
  - **Opinion** 
  - **Expository**
- Practice SPO scaffolding activities:
  1. Create an SPO with the whole class.
  2. Distinguish a Topic Sentence (T.S.) from details.
  3. Identify the T.S. and sequence details.
  4. Given a T.S., select relevant details and eliminate irrelevant detail(s) from a list or SPO.
  5. Given a T.S., generate details.
  6. Practice two or three ways of writing T.S. or C.S. (if appositives introduced).
  7. Given topic/prompt, generate a T.S.
  8. Given a T.S. and details, generate a C.S.
  9. Given topic/prompt, construct an SPO independently.
  10. Draft a paragraph based on an SPO.

### Revising and Editing

- Brainstorm more specific and vivid vocabulary to replace vague or overused words.
- Improve given topic and concluding sentences by making them more specific and engaging.
- Revise unelaborated paragraphs using explicit instructions, first as a class activity and then in pairs or small groups.
- Revise drafts based on explicit feedback from teachers and peers.
- Proofread and edit writing for proper use of:
  - Commas
  - Capitalization 
  - Punctuation
  - Complete sentences (fixing fragments and run-ons)
  - Spelling

### TO, MPO, and Compositions

Students should be introduced to the MPO and the activities that follow.
Students who are not ready for the MPO can continue to use the TO but with
the same topic as the rest of the class.

- Create an MPO as a whole class.
- Introduce the general, specific, and thesis statement format for
introductions for MPOs.
- Introduce the thesis, specific, and general statement format for
conclusions for MPOs.
- Practice writing a plan of development for some MPOs.
- Develop a draft from an MPO.
- Write introductions and conclusions independently for compositions
from an MPO.
- Revise and edit drafts based on MPOs.
- Create MPOs for the following text structures:
  - narrative
  - problem/solution
  - compare and contrast
  - cause/effect
  - pro/con
- Given a debatable topic, research both sides and develop a thesis
statement for a pro/con essay.
- Sequence claims and counterclaims for a pro/con composition.
- Draft, revise, and edit a pro/con composition from an MPO

### Research Paper

- Introduce and practice the research paper process through teacher modeling and whole class activities, starting with biographical models.

#### Part 1: Background Research and Planning
- Read and annotate background articles
- Gather and evaluate primary and secondary sources from academic databases
- Identify and underline key words and phrases in sources
- Create Works Cited page following proper citation format
- Develop research plan and thesis statement
- Take structured notes from sources using index cards
- Organize and sequence index cards according to research plan

#### Part 2: Writing and Development
- Convert index card notes into MPO details
- Draft research paper introduction incorporating thesis
- Transform MPO into complete research paper

#### Research Paper Models
Practice the complete research process using these models:
- Biographical research papers
- Significant historical events
- Problem/issue analysis
- Argumentative research
- Compare and contrast studies
