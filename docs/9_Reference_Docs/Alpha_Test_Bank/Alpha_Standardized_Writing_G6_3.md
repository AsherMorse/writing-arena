## Reading Passage: Monarchs on the Move

**Paragraph 1**  
For his ninth birthday, Carter Steadman didn't want toys or games. Instead, he asked for donations to help monarch butterflies. Carter wanted to build a way stationâ€”a small garden where monarchs could rest and lay eggs. With his parents' help, he cleared part of his backyard in Virginia and planted rows of milkweed, the butterflies' favorite food. Then he waited for the monarchs to come.

**Paragraph 2**  
Every fall, millions of monarch butterflies fly thousands of miles from Canada and the United States to the mountains of Mexico. There they spend the winter resting in trees. When spring arrives, new generations begin the long journey north again, stopping to eat and lay eggs along the way. It is one of the most remarkable migrations in nature.

**Paragraph 3**  
In recent years, however, fewer butterflies have completed this journey. People have cleared land for farms and lawns, destroying milkweed plants that monarchs depend on. Strong storms have also damaged the forests where the butterflies spend the winter. Scientists worry that the monarch population is shrinking.

**Paragraph 4**  
To help, thousands of people across North America have become citizen scientists. They tag butterflies with small stickers, count caterpillars, and record monarch sightings online. These reports help scientists understand how the butterflies are doing and where they need protection.

**Paragraph 5**  
Carter continues to raise and tag monarchs in his backyard. He believes that small actions can make a big difference. "Humans may be the problem," he says, "but we're also the solution." Thanks to helpers like Carter, monarchs may have a better chance to survive.

*(Adapted from SuperScience April 2017)*

## Essay Prompt

Write an **expository essay** explaining how people are helping monarch butterflies survive.

Use information from the passage to support your ideas.

### Make sure your response includes the following:

**Write 5 clear paragraphs:** Introduction with a strong thesis, 3 body paragraphs with topic sentences, and a conclusion (5 points)

**Use evidence and explain it:** Support each point with facts or examples from the passage and explain what they show (5 points)

**Stay organized and on topic:** Use transitions, keep your ideas in order, and focus on the main idea (4 points)

**Use strong sentences:** Vary sentence types and use clear, formal language (3 points)

**Edit your writing:** Check your spelling, punctuation, and grammar (3 points)
