Hi there! Today we're going to learn about a concept in English called _appositives_. These are words or groups of words that give more info about nouns in sentences.

## What's an Appositive?

An appositive is a noun or a group of words that sits next to another noun to describe it, explain it, or rename it. Here's an example:

"New York City, the biggest city in the USA, is famous for tourists."

In this sentence, "the biggest city in the USA" is an appositive that tells us more about "New York City."

## How to Do This Activity:

There are three types of appositive activities.

1. **Fill in the appositive:** Given a sentence, fill in an appositive that makes sense.
2. **Write a sentence using an appositive:** Write a sentence that uses the appositive provided.
3. **Write a sentence about a topic that uses an appositive:** Write a sentence about a topic that uses an appositive. Details about what kind of appositive may also be provided.

We're looking at appositives that don't have verbs in them for this activity.

## Kinds of Appositives

There are two kinds of appositives:

- **Non-Essential Appositives:** These are surrounded by commas and provide additional information that is not necessary to understand a sentence. If you remove them from a sentence, the sentence can still be understood.

- **Essential Appositives:** These are usually not surrounded by commas and contain information that is necessary to fully understand a sentence. If you remove them from a sentence, the sentence can no longer be fully understood.

## Examples to Help You Understand

### Appositives at the Start or End of Sentences

- **A big fan of books,** Mary spends lots of time in libraries. (Here, "A big fan of books" is about Mary.)
- I love my dog, **a friendly golden retriever**. ("A friendly golden retriever" gives more information about the dog.)

### Sentences Without Appositives

- The sun is very bright.
- Cats like to sleep a lot.

### Essential Appositives

- My teacher **Mrs. Johnson** is very kind. (No commas, because "Mrs. Johnson" is needed to know which teacher.)
- The book **Harry Potter** is very popular. ("Harry Potter" is needed to know which book.)

## Example Sentences with Explainers

Here are example appositives in these sentences:

1. French fries, **a well-liked snack,** were first made in Belgium.

   - "A well-liked snack" is the appositive because it tells us more about "French fries."

2. Let's eat at that place, **where they make real pizzas.**

   - "where they make real pizzas" tells us something special about the restaurant.

3. India, famous for many cultures and traditions, is where yoga started, **a practice for mind and body.**

   - "A practice for mind and body" tells us what yoga is.

4. Italy, **a country known for art,** has twenty areas including Sicily, **an island in the sea.**

   - The first appositive talks about Italy, and the second about Sicily.

5. My coach **John** showed me how to play basketball.
   - "John" gives more info about the coach. It's an essential appositive, so there are no commas around it.
