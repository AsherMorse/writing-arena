## Reading Passage: A Special Teacher

Kinsley read about a teacher in Africa and wrote this paper to tell what she learned. Read Kinsley's paper and look for the revisions she needs to make. Then answer the questions that follow.

### A Special Teacher

**1** In 2019, teachers from around the world gathered for a significant event. **2** In this event, Science teacher Peter Tabichi received an award. **3** The award he received was the prestigious Global Teacher Prize. **4** His dedication to education and his impact on students' lives. **5** As part of this recognition, he will receive $1 million over ten years.

**6** Peter Tabichi is from Kenya, Africa, and teaches high school science. **7** He initially worked at a private school with ample resources. **8** In 2016, he visited a small public school with minimal supplies, only one computer, and no reliable internet. **9** Some students had to walk four miles to school. **10** If he wanted to make a difference.

**11** Recognizing the hardships his students faced, such as hunger and lack of school supplies, Tabichi used his own salary to provide food and materials for them. **12** He even taught them how to grow drought-resistant crops. **13** His dedication has transformed the school. **14** Due to his efforts, enrollment has doubled, and more students are now attending college. **15** With his award money, he plans to continue improving the school, believing that Africa will produce future scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs recognized worldwide.

## Questions

### Question 2

Sentence 4 is a fragment. Expand it into a complete sentence.

**4** His dedication to education and his impact on students' lives.

### Question 3

Rewrite the sentence below to add an appositive that gives more information about Peter Tabichi.

**6** Peter Tabichi is from Kenya, Africa, and teaches high school science.

### Question 4

Sentence 10 starts with a subordinating conjunction, but it is incomplete.

**10** If he wanted to make a difference.

Rewrite it as a complete sentence by completing the thought.

### Question 5

Combine sentences 13 and 14 into a single sentence using because, but, or so to show how the ideas are connected.

**13** His dedication has transformed the school. **14** Due to his efforts, enrollment has doubled, and more students are now attending college.

### Question 6

Have you ever faced a challenge at school that made learning difficult? Write a sentence to share your experience.

### Question 7

What lesson from Peter Tabichi's story do you think applies to your own life? Write a sentence to share your thoughts.

### Question 8

What is one way you can show kindness to others in your daily life? Write one sentence to share your idea.

### Question 9

If you won a big award, how would you use the prize money to help others? Write a sentence to share your idea.

### Question 10

What is a skill or talent you have that could help others in your community? Write one sentence to share your idea.
