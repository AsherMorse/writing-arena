## Reading Passage: The Texas Bluebonnet

**1** Every spring, Texans look forward to the little blue dots that pop up in fields. **2** As days go by, the dots seem to grow in number. **3** Roadsides and hills take on a whole new look. **4** Meadows do too. **5** Soon, a beautiful blue blanket covers the land. **6** It is bluebonnet season in Texas, and everyone loves it.

**7** A wildflower with bright blue petals. **8** Its color is between navy and royal blue. **9** When grouped together, bluebonnets look especially bright and pretty.

**10** Since bluebonnets grow best in sunny places. **11** Surprisingly, they can survive cold Texas spring weather and don't need much water or fertilizer. **12** They're strong, tough flowers.

**13** But the bluebonnet is more than just beautiful — it's also the state flower of Texas. **14** That makes it a big part of Texas pride. **15** People wear shirts, drink from mugs, and buy candles with bluebonnets on them. **16** The flower is also in songs and stories.

**17** The bluebonnet is an annual flower, blooming just once a year — usually from March to early May. **18** During this brief window, people flock to the fields to take photos surrounded by the blossoms. **19** Many people attend bluebonnet festivals in small towns all over Texas.
